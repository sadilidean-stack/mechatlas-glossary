AC Circuits Containing Resistance Only

A type of AC circuit where only resistors are present.

AC Cycles

One full pattern of an alternating waveform.

Ackerman Steering Mechanism

A steering geometry ensuring all wheels follow concentric paths during a turn.

Additive Manufacturing

A manufacturing process that builds objects layer by layer from digital models.

Addressing & Tag-Based Programming

Uses fixed memory locations or named tags to improve readability and troubleshooting.

Analog-to-Digital Conversion (ADC)

Process of converting analog sensor signals into digital values.

ALU — Arithmetic Logic Unit

Performs arithmetic and logical operations on binary data.

Analog Signal Scaling

Adjusting sensor outputs to fit the required input range of a controller or ADC.

AND Gate

A logic gate that outputs HIGH only if all inputs are HIGH.

Alternating Current

Electrical current that periodically reverses direction; AC is widely used for power distribution and transformation.

Alternating Current Transmission

Delivering electrical power over long distances using AC with transformers to reduce losses.

Automatic Retraction Sequence

A programmed pneumatic sequence where a cylinder retracts automatically after a delay.

Automatic Valve Control

Valve actuated automatically (solenoid, pilot, sensor) for precise flow and actuator control.

Batch Production

Manufacturing method where products are made in groups or batches, balancing efficiency and flexibility.

Block Diagram

A simplified graphical representation of a system that uses blocks to show components and arrows to show signal flow.

BUSES (Data, Address, Control)

Electrical pathways for transferring data, addresses, and control signals in microprocessor systems.

CAD/CAM Integration

Combining computer-aided design and manufacturing to streamline product design and automated production.

CANopen Node Communication

Protocol enabling embedded devices to communicate on a CAN network with standardized profiles.

Capacitance

Property allowing components to store energy in an electric field, resisting sudden voltage changes.

Circuit

A closed path that allows current to flow, distributing energy to devices and systems.

Closed-loop Control

System where output is continuously measured and corrected to match the desired input.

Communication Protocols

Rules governing data exchange between devices, ensuring correct serial, I2C, SPI, or wireless communication.

Control Logic Sequences

Step-by-step logical operations performed by the PLC, used for motor circuits, conveyors, filling systems, and safety shutdowns.

Control Loops

Systems that monitor process variables, compare with setpoints, and adjust manipulated variables to maintain stability and accuracy.

CPU — Central Processing Unit

Main processor that fetches, decodes, and executes instructions, coordinating all operations within the computer.

CU — Control Unit

Manages CPU activities, generating timing and control signals, and directing data flow during the fetch–decode–execute cycle.

Current

Flow of electric charge through a conductor, powering devices and measured in amperes (A).

Current and Voltage in Resistive Circuits

In purely resistive AC circuits, voltage and current follow each other exactly, governed by Ohm's Law (I = V/R).

DC and AC Current Flows

DC flows steadily in one direction, while AC alternates direction repeatedly, affecting measurement and circuit behavior.

Digital and Analog I/O Interfacing

Techniques for reading digital/analog inputs and controlling outputs, enabling switches, LEDs, buzzers, and ADC/PWM signals.

Digital Signal Interpretation

Converts raw digital sensor outputs into meaningful data for reliable system integration.

Digital vs. Analog I/O

Digital signals are ON/OFF (1/0), while analog signals vary continuously (e.g., 0–10V, 4–20mA) for real-world interfacing.

Driving Digital Outputs

Controlling devices like LEDs or buzzers using microcontroller pins to indicate status or actuate systems.

Electrical Latching Circuit

Circuit that maintains output state after input is removed, commonly used in automation for continuous operation.

Embedded Programming (C / Arduino)

Programming microcontrollers with C or Arduino to control pins, sensors, actuators, and implement logic.

EMF of Self-Induction

Voltage generated in a coil opposing current change, fundamental to inductors, chokes, and transformers.

EtherCAT Data Exchange

High-performance, real-time Ethernet-based protocol enabling synchronized, low-latency communication for automation and robotics.

Feedback-Based Actuation

A control method where actuators move only after receiving confirmatory sensor signals.

Fixed Resistors

Resistors with constant resistance used to limit current and divide voltage.

Forward Bias Operation

Condition where a diode conducts electricity freely, allowing current to pass when correctly polarized.

Friction

Resistive force when two surfaces move against each other, converting kinetic energy into heat and enabling grip.

Full-Step Mode

Full-step mode is a stepper motor driving method where the motor moves one full step per pulse, energizing one or two motor windings at a time to achieve the standard step angle.

Full-Wave Rectification

Converts both halves of an AC waveform to DC using a bridge diode configuration, producing smoother output.

Half-Step Mode

Stepper motor mode alternating between single-phase and dual-phase excitation, halving the step angle compared to full-step mode.

Half-Wave Rectification

Converts AC to DC using a single diode, producing pulsating DC output.

Heat Action

Thermal energy transfer driving expansion, phase changes, and energy conversion.

Inductance in DC Circuits

Opposition to current change in DC circuits due to inductors, active during switching.

Inductive Sensor Triggering

Detects metal objects using electromagnetic fields, sending trigger signals to PLC inputs.

Interlocking for Safety

Prevents unsafe machine operation by enforcing safety conditions before activation.

Just-In-Time (JIT) Production

Production strategy that reduces inventory by producing only when needed.

Kinematics

Mechanics branch analyzing motion without considering forces.

Kinematics Diagram

Simplified mechanism representation showing links and joints for motion analysis.

Ladder Logic & Boolean Operations

Graphical programming language resembling relay logic diagrams using AND, OR, NOT, NAND/NOR, and XOR operations for sequences, interlocks, timers, and safety logic.

Lean Manufacturing

Production philosophy focused on eliminating waste and maximizing value.

LED Colors and Wavelengths

LEDs emit colors based on semiconductor material, used in displays and lighting.

LED Polarity

Correct anode/cathode connection ensures LED lights up properly in circuits.

Load‑tracking Capability

Servo motor system adapts output when load changes, maintaining performance.

Manual Valve Operation

The valve is operated directly by a human using lever, push button, or other manual device to shift the valve spool or change flow paths in pneumatic or hydraulic systems.

Mechanical Action

The motion or force generated by a mechanical system or mechanism during operation. Enables machines to perform work, transmit forces, and carry out mechanical tasks.

Mechanical Drive

System of components (gears, belts, chains, shafts) that transmits power and motion from a motor or engine to machine parts. Delivers required speed, torque, and motion to the load for proper machine operation.

Memory Function in Double Solenoid Valves

Double solenoid valves can retain their position after the control signal is removed, reducing operator intervention and automating sequences.

Microcontroller Architecture

Internal structure of microcontrollers including CPU, memory, and I/O ports, explaining how they process data and interact with peripherals.

Microcontroller-Based System Design

Creating complete embedded systems integrating hardware and software, covering power regulation, PCB layout, testing, and software–hardware integration.

Modbus RTU

A serial-based industrial communication protocol (master‑slave) using binary-encoded messages with CRC error checking for low‑cost communication between PLCs and field devices.

Modbus TCP

A version of Modbus that wraps the standard protocol into TCP/IP packets, allowing Modbus communication over Ethernet networks instead of serial lines.

Motion

The change in position of an object with respect to time, a fundamental concept in mechanics describing how systems move.

NAND Gate (NOT-AND)

A logic gate that outputs the negation of the AND operation, producing LOW only if all inputs are HIGH; otherwise, output is HIGH.

Noise Elimination

Techniques applied during sensor signal conditioning such as filtering, differential signaling, and shielding to reduce unwanted noise from sensor signals.

NOR Gate (NOT-OR)

A logic gate that outputs the negation of the OR operation, giving HIGH only when all inputs are LOW.

NOT Gate (Inverter)

A basic logic gate with a single input that outputs the logical negation: input 1 → output 0; input 0 → output 1.

Ohm's Law

Fundamental relationship between voltage, current, and resistance: I = V / R, essential for designing and analyzing circuits.

Off-Delay Timer Function

An off-delay timer keeps the output ON even after the input turns OFF, only switching OFF after the preset time expires, used when actuators must stay energized briefly.

On-Delay Timer Function

An on-delay timer activates its output only after the input signal has been ON for a set time, used to slow down activation of solenoids or relays in pneumatic sequences.

Open-Loop vs. Closed-Loop Control

Open-loop: control based on input alone without monitoring output; Closed-loop: continuously monitors output and adjusts inputs to reduce error using feedback.

OR Gate

A basic digital logic gate that implements logical disjunction, outputting HIGH (1) if at least one input is HIGH (1).

Parallel LED Circuits

Multiple LEDs connected across the same voltage source so each LED receives the same voltage; current divides among LEDs and one failing does not stop others.

Parallel Resistors

Resistors connected across the same two nodes; currents split and equivalent resistance is less than the smallest individual resistor.

PN Junction Diode Theory

Fundamental semiconductor junction formed by P-type and N-type materials; conducts easily forward and blocks in reverse, forming the basis of diode behavior.

Power Factor

Cosine of the phase angle between voltage and current; indicates how effectively electrical power is converted into useful work.

Power in Resistive Circuits

Energy consumption in purely resistive circuits where power is P = V × I and voltage and current are in phase.

Power Transmission

The process of transferring energy from a source to a load, enabling machines to operate by delivering mechanical or electrical power.

Pressure

Force exerted per unit area on a surface; a fundamental parameter in fluid mechanics and hydraulics, measured in pascals (Pa).

Pressure Compensation

Adjusting or controlling pressure so that changes in system demand or load do not cause pressure to exceed or drop below certain values, or using compensating mechanisms or valves to maintain stable pressure.

Pressure Regulation

The act of maintaining a desired pressure level or limiting pressure within a fluid‑power system or part of it.

Pressure Control in Fluid Power

Techniques and devices to regulate pressure within hydraulic and pneumatic systems to ensure safe, reliable, and correct actuator operation.

PROFINET Cyclic Data

Industrial‑Ethernet mechanism for periodic exchange of I/O data between controllers and devices to support real‑time automation tasks.

Proximity Sensor Detection

Non‑contact sensing method to detect nearby objects for presence or position signals used in automation sequencing.

Process Flow Analysis

Method for studying and mapping processes to identify bottlenecks and optimize workflows for improved manufacturing efficiency.

Process Variable (PV)

The actual measured value of the quantity being controlled (e.g., temperature, pressure, level, speed); used as feedback for control actions.

Pulse Width Modulation (PWM)

Control technique using a train of digital pulses with variable duty cycle to encode desired power, position, or speed for motors and LEDs.

Quality Assurance

Systematic set of process‑focused activities intended to prevent defects by defining and maintaining planned processes and standards.

Quality Control

Inspection and testing activities used to detect and correct defects in products and processes to meet specified quality requirements.

Rack/Chassis vs. Compact PLCs

Rack/chassis PLCs are modular with separate slots for CPU, power and I/O for large systems; compact PLCs combine CPU, power and I/O in one housing for smaller machines.

Reading Digital Inputs

Technique for detecting state of switches, push-buttons and discrete sensors connected to microcontroller or PLC input pins to trigger logic.

Reed Switch Cylinder Positioning

Magnetic reed switch mounted on a cylinder to detect piston position and provide discrete feedback for sequencing and control.

REGISTERS

Very fast storage locations inside a CPU used to hold instructions, operands and addresses temporarily during processing.

Resistance

Opposition to current flow in a conductor or component, measured in ohms (Ω), used to control current and create voltage drops.

Resistor Tolerance

Maximum allowable deviation of a resistor's actual value from its nominal value, expressed as a percentage; important for circuit accuracy.

Reverse Bias Operation

Diode condition where the anode is at lower potential than the cathode, causing the diode to block forward current and pass only small leakage current.

Review of Resistance in AC Circuits

Behavior of resistive elements in AC circuits: they oppose current and dissipate energy as heat without introducing phase shift between voltage and current.

Rotary Power

Power delivered through rotational motion.

Rotational Speed

The number of rotations per unit time, often measured in RPM.

Safety Interlocks & Fail-Safe Controls

Logical and hardware measures (emergency stop, guard interlocks, overload protection) that prevent dangerous machine states and protect personnel and equipment.

Self-Holding Relay Operation

Relay wiring arrangement that latches its coil circuit after activation so the output remains energized even when the initiating signal is removed.

Sensor Calibration

Process of adjusting a sensor's output or data processing to match a known reference, correcting offsets and scaling for accurate measurements over time.

Sensor-to-Relay Interface

Interface technique allowing low-power sensor outputs to drive relays or other final elements to switch higher-power loads.

Sensors and Actuators Integration

Design practice for reading sensors and controlling actuators within embedded or PLC systems to achieve coordinated system behavior.

Series LED Circuits

LEDs connected end-to-end so the same current flows through all devices and source voltage is divided among them; used when matched brightness is required.

Series Resistors

Resistors connected in line so the same current flows through each; total resistance equals the sum of individual values and is used to limit current or create voltage drops.

Signal Conditioning & Electrical Isolation

Techniques to scale, filter and isolate sensor signals (often via conditioners or optocouplers) to protect controllers and improve measurement quality.

Single-loop vs Multi-loop control

Single-loop control uses one feedback loop for one variable; multi-loop (cascade or hierarchical) uses nested or parallel loops to control interdependent variables for improved performance.

Shielding Technique

Use of shielded cables, grounded enclosures, twisted-pair wiring and proper grounding to protect signals from EMI and preserve measurement integrity.

Six Sigma

Methodology for improving processes by reducing defects and variability using statistical tools and disciplined problem‑solving.

Speed/Position Correction

Servo control process using feedback to detect deviation between desired and actual speed or position and to command corrective motor actions until error is minimized.

Step Angle

Step angle is the angular movement a stepper motor makes per pulse, typically calculated using step angle = 360° / (number of steps per revolution).

Stop Circuit (NC Contact)

A stop circuit uses a normally closed (NC) switch to immediately cut power to a device. When pressed, it interrupts the current flow, stopping machine operation. This is a critical safety feature in industrial automation.

Subtractive Manufacturing

Manufacturing approach where material is removed (machining, milling, drilling) from a workpiece to form the desired shape.

Time-Based Cylinder Extension

Controlling cylinder extension based on a fixed timer rather than position sensing; useful for repetitive operations requiring a fixed stroke duration.

Timer-Controlled Relay Switching

Relays switched by timer outputs to automate timed actuation of solenoids, motors, lamps or other devices.

Voltage

Voltage is the electric potential difference between two points in a circuit; it provides the driving force that pushes electrons through a conductor.

Waveforms

Graphical representations of voltage or current versus time that show signal shapes such as sine, square and triangular waves.

Why DC Cannot Measure AC

DC measurement instruments expect steady signals; rapidly varying AC requires specialized techniques and instruments to obtain meaningful readings.

3/2 Directional Control Valve Operation

Valve with three ports and two positions used to control single-acting actuators by connecting supply to the actuator or venting it to exhaust.

4/2 Directional Control Valve Operation

Valve with four ports and two positions typically used with double-acting actuators to alternate supply and exhaust, enabling extend/retract motion.

4/3 Directional Control Valve Operation

Four-port, three-position valve offering extend, retract and a neutral center state that can block, vent or recirculate to hold or secure actuators.

5/2 Directional Control Valve Operation

Five-port, two-position valve commonly used in pneumatics to alternate supply to either side of a double-acting cylinder while exhausting the opposite side.
